Relocating to a new country involves finding the right environment for both your personal life and professional productivity. We help you transition smoothly by organizing targeted scouting trips and settling-in support.

## Targeted Scouting Trips

Before committing to a long-term lease, we recommend a scouting trip to experience potential regions first-hand. Our travel specialist, Manuela Broccolini, designs custom itineraries tailored to remote workers:

*   **Connectivity Checks:** Evaluating neighborhood internet reliability and checking cell signal strength.
*   **Infrastructure Audits:** Identifying local co-working spaces, cafes with reliable Wi-Fi, and public transit links.
*   **Lifestyle Assessment:** Experiencing neighborhood walkability, access to gyms, grocery stores, and local community hubs.
*   **Property Viewings:** Coordinating visits with local rental agents to preview available apartments.

## Language & Cultural Integration

Integrating into your new community requires more than just a visa. Speaking the local language is key to handling daily tasks, making friends, and participating in Italian life.

*   **Italian School of San Diego:** We provide structured Italian language classes (private or group, remote or in-person) tailored to your proficiency level.
*   **Practical Vocabulary:** Courses emphasize conversational skills for dealing with utilities, landlords, municipal offices, and daily shopping.
*   **Cultural Coaching:** We offer workshops to help you understand local customs, bureaucracy etiquette, and business culture.

## Settling-in Support

When you arrive, our local logistics team helps you settle in quickly to minimize work downtime:
*   **Mobile Services:** Setting up local SIM cards with high data limits (e.g., Iliad, Vodafone, TIM).
*   **Pet Relocation:** Guiding you through the EU Pet Passport requirements, microchipping, and USDA certifications.
*   **Local Introductions:** Connecting you with local expat networks and remote worker communities in your chosen area.
